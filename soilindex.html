Soil Health Card Scheme in India

The Soil Health Card (SHC) scheme is a significant initiative related to soil health and soil index in India. It aims to assess and improve the soil fertility and nutrient content in agricultural fields across the country.

Key Points about the Soil Health Card Scheme:

  • 1
    Objective: The primary objective of the Soil Health Card scheme is to provide accurate information to farmers about the nutrient status of their soil.
  • 2
    Issuance of Soil Health Cards: Under this scheme, soil samples are collected from farmers' fields, and these samples are then tested in soil testing laboratories.
  • 3
    Information on Soil Health Cards: The Soil Health Cards contain essential information, including nutrient status, recommendations, pH levels, and more.
  • 4
    Benefits for Farmers: Improved crop yields, cost savings, and sustainable agriculture are some of the key benefits.
  • 5
    Coverage: The Soil Health Card scheme is a nationwide initiative covering all states and union territories in India.
  • 6
    Frequency of Testing: Soil testing and issuance of Soil Health Cards are generally done periodically.
  • 7
    Digital Initiatives: Some states have developed mobile apps and online portals for easy access to Soil Health Card information.
  • 8
    Government Support: The Indian government provides financial support to states for effective implementation.

The Soil Health Card scheme plays a crucial role in promoting sustainable agriculture, reducing input costs for farmers, and improving overall agricultural productivity by addressing soil health issues. It is one of the key initiatives aimed at ensuring the long-term health and fertility of Indian soils.
